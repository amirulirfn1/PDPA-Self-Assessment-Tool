PDPA Act Guidelines

Trusted sources to learn about Malaysia's PDPA Act

Malaysian Litigator

This article outlines several steps businesses need to take to comply with the PDPA. Key tips include appointing a Compliance Person to manage data protection issues, developing a code of practice, sending out PDPA notices to data subjects, and ensuring data integrity and security through standard operating procedures (SOPs). The article also discusses the penalties for non-compliance​.

Azmilaw

This resource details the seven fundamental principles of the PDPA: General, Notice and Choice, Disclosure, Security, Retention, Data Integrity, and Access principles. It highlights common breaches and provides minimum standards for compliance with the Security, Retention, and Data Integrity principles. Practical steps for businesses include establishing access control, maintaining proper ID and password management, and ensuring the secure storage and disposal of documents​

Data Guidance

The DataGuidance website provides an overview of Malaysia's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). It offers detailed insights into the regulatory framework, key definitions, data protection principles, rights of individuals, and obligations of data users under the Malaysian PDPA. The guide also covers topics such as data transfers, security measures, and enforcement actions, helping organizations understand their compliance requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the PDPA Act and our self-assessment tool.

  • What is the PDPA Act?

    The PDPA Act is a law that governs the collection, use, and protection of personal data in Malaysia. It aims to ensure that personal data is handled responsibly and individuals' privacy is safeguarded.

  • Why is compliance with the PDPA Act important?

    Compliance with the PDPA Act is crucial as it helps protect personal data, prevents data breaches, and ensures that organizations are following legal requirements. Non-compliance can result in significant fines and damage to reputation.

  • How does the self-assessment tool work?

    The self-assessment tool provides a series of questions that help you evaluate your compliance with the PDPA Act. Based on your responses, it calculates your overall compliance score and identifies areas that may need improvement.

  • How can I access my assessment results?

    After completing the assessment, your results will be displayed on the assessment results page. You can also log in to your profile at any time to view and track your assessment history.

  • Where can I find more information about the PDPA Act?

    You can find more information about the PDPA Act on the official government website or by consulting legal resources that specialize in data protection and privacy laws.
